Taj Mahal, Agra

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is a stunning white marble mausoleum built by Emperor Shah Jahan in 1632 as a symbol of love for his wife, Mumtaz Mahal. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Wonders of the World.

Stone Chariot, Hampi

The Stone Chariot in Hampi, Karnataka, is an iconic 16th-century granite structure located within the Vittala Temple complex. Resembling a chariot, it is a masterpiece of Vijayanagara architecture and symbolizes Karnataka's rich cultural heritage, often compared to the Sun Temple's chariot in Konark.

Mysore Palace, Karnataka

The Mysore Palace, located in Karnataka, India, is a stunning example of Indo-Saracenic architecture and the official residence of the Wadiyar dynasty. Built in 1912, it features intricate carvings, domes, and arches, with a grand Durbar Hall and a dazzling display of lights during the annual Dasara festival.

Chola Temples,Tamil Nadu

The Chola Temples in Tamil Nadu, including Brihadeeswarar, Gangaikonda Cholapuram, and Airavatesvara, are UNESCO World Heritage Sites renowned for their grand Dravidian architecture and intricate sculptures.

Red Fort,Delhi

The Red Fort, located in Delhi, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1648. Made of red sandstone, it symbolizes Mughal grandeur and hosts India's Independence Day celebrations annually.
